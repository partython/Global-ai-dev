# Acceptable Use Policy

**Last Updated:** March 2026

**Effective Date:** March 1, 2026

**Version:** 1.0

## 1. Introduction and Scope

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") establishes guidelines for proper and lawful use of the Priya Global Platform ("Service"). This policy applies to all users, customers, and integrations accessing the Service. Violation of this AUP may result in account suspension, termination, and potential legal action.

The AUP supplements the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy. In case of conflict, the most restrictive provision applies. Priya AI Technologies ("Company," "we," "us") reserves the right to modify this policy at any time with 30 days notice.

## 2. Prohibited Activities

### 2.1 Illegal Activities
Users shall not use the Service for:
- Violating any local, state, national, or international law
- Facilitating criminal activity or fraud
- Money laundering or terrorist financing
- Tax evasion or regulatory violations
- Sanctions violations or export control violations
- Espionage or unauthorized government access
- Identity theft, forgery, or impersonation
- Any activity declared illegal by applicable jurisdiction

**Enforcement:** Illegal content triggers immediate account suspension, legal reporting, and evidence preservation.

### 2.2 Intellectual Property Violation
Users shall not:
- Infringe copyright through unauthorized distribution
- Use trademarked content without permission
- Misappropriate trade secrets or confidential information
- Violate patents or proprietary rights
- Plagiarize or claim others' work as own
- Reverse engineer licensed software
- Remove or alter copyright notices and licenses
- Sell or distribute copyrighted content

**Enforcement:** DMCA notices honored; accounts suspended for repeated violations; legal claims supported.

### 2.3 Malware and Security Threats
Users shall not:
- Upload or distribute viruses, worms, or Trojans
- Distribute ransomware or spyware
- Create or distribute botnet malware
- Distribute rootkits or kernel exploits
- Attempt to bypass security controls
- Probe for security vulnerabilities
- Conduct port scanning or network reconnaissance
- Attempt privilege escalation
- Weaponize the Service

**Enforcement:** Accounts permanently terminated for malware distribution; legal action pursued for serious threats.

### 2.4 Spam and Unsolicited Communications
Users shall not:
- Send unsolicited bulk emails or SMS messages
- Use for email bombing or DDoS spam
- Send spam via Platform messaging features
- Harvest email addresses from the Platform
- Use misleading sender information or spoofing
- Create disposable accounts for spam distribution
- Manipulate algorithms to achieve spam distribution
- Bypass consent requirements for messaging

**Enforcement:** Rate limiting, account suspension, and termination for repeat violations; ISP/telecom complaints filed.

### 2.5 Phishing and Social Engineering
Users shall not:
- Send phishing emails impersonating legitimate entities
- Create fake login pages or credential harvesting forms
- Send SMS phishing or smishing attacks
- Use social engineering to gain unauthorized access
- Impersonate Company employees or support staff
- Create misleading landing pages
- Conduct whaling attacks on executives
- Distribute keylogging or credential theft tools

**Enforcement:** Immediate account termination; cooperation with law enforcement; fraud reports filed.

### 2.6 Harassment and Abuse
Users shall not:
- Send threatening, harassing, or abusive messages
- Engage in cyberbullying or targeted harassment
- Send unwanted sexual content or solicitation
- Stalk, dox, or threaten other users
- Coordinate group harassment campaigns
- Send hate speech or discriminatory content
- Threaten violence or harm
- Engage in reputation attacks or defamation

**Enforcement:** Account suspension for first violation; termination for repeated or severe cases.

### 2.7 Child Exploitation and CSAM
Users absolutely shall not:
- Distribute child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
- Solicit or produce CSAM
- Groom or exploit minors
- Share inappropriate content with minors
- Facilitate child trafficking
- Use Service for child endangerment

**Enforcement:** Immediate permanent account termination; evidence reported to NCMEC and law enforcement; legal prosecution pursued.

### 2.8 Non-Consensual Intimate Content
Users shall not:
- Distribute intimate images without consent (revenge porn)
- Deepfake intimate imagery of non-consenting persons
- Threaten disclosure of intimate content
- Create non-consensual synthetic media
- Blackmail using intimate content
- Harass individuals through intimate image distribution

**Enforcement:** Immediate account suspension; content removal; legal cooperation with authorities.

Users shall:

Users shall not:
- Use AI to generate fraudulent documents
- Deploy AI to impersonate real individuals
- Use AI to circumvent security controls
- Generate AI-powered spam, malware, or phishing
- Use AI to facilitate harassment or abuse
- Deploy AI to violate intellectual property rights

### 3.2 AI Output Standards
The Company disclaims responsibility for:
- Factual accuracy of AI outputs
- Appropriateness of AI-generated content
- Bias or discrimination in AI responses
- Misuse of AI features by users

Users assume full responsibility for:
- Reviewing and validating AI outputs
- Compliance implications of AI content
- Legal consequences of AI deployment
- Consequences of AI errors or bias

### 3.3 AI Training and Data Usage

## 4. Rate Limits and Fair Usage

### 4.1 Standard Rate Limits
Default rate limits per account:
- **API Requests:** 1,000 requests/minute
- **Message Sending:** 10,000 messages/hour per channel
- **Concurrent Connections:** 100 active connections
- **File Uploads:** 100 MB per upload, 10 GB per day
- **Database Queries:** 500 queries/minute
- **Webhook Deliveries:** 10,000 per minute

### 4.2 Burst Limits
Temporary bursts permitted within reason:
- Brief spikes 150% of sustained limit acceptable
- Sustained usage above limits requires upgrade
- Repeated burst abuse triggers rate limiting
- Excessive bursts trigger account review

### 4.3 Rate Limit Responses
When limits exceeded:
- **First Violation:** 429 (Too Many Requests) status returned
- **Continued Violation:** Automatic rate limiting applied
- **Persistent Abuse:** Account suspension pending review
- **Enterprise Plans:** Custom limits negotiated per contract

### 4.4 Fair Usage Enforcement
The Company reserves right to:
- Suspend accounts exceeding reasonable usage
- Require plan upgrades for heavy usage
- Terminate accounts repeatedly violating limits
- Redirect abusive traffic
- Implement progressive rate limiting

### 4.5 Legitimate High-Volume Use
Enterprise customers may:
- Negotiate custom rate limits
- Implement tiered scaling per agreement
- Request volume commitments
- Receive dedicated infrastructure
- Scale within agreed parameters

## 5. Spam and Messaging Compliance

### 5.1 Anti-Spam Laws
Users shall comply with anti-spam regulations:

**CAN-SPAM Act (USA)**
- Obtain clear consent before marketing emails
- Honor unsubscribe requests within 10 days
- Include accurate sender information
- Provide physical postal address
- Use truthful subject lines

**GDPR (EU/UK)**
- Obtain explicit opt-in before marketing
- Maintain consent records
- Honor withdrawal of consent immediately
- Provide clear unsubscribe mechanisms
- Document lawful processing basis

**CASL (Canada)**
- Obtain express or implied consent before commercial messages
- Clearly identify sender
- Provide unsubscribe mechanism
- Honor opt-out within 10 days
- Maintain consent records

**PIPEDA (Canada)**
- Only use data for stated purposes
- Obtain consent for new uses
- Secure Personal Information
- Respond to access requests

**LGPD (Brazil)**
- Obtain consent for most processing
- Provide data subject rights
- Maintain privacy notice
- Report breaches to ANPD
- Appoint data protection officer

### 5.2 Messaging Best Practices
Users shall:
- Obtain explicit consent before messaging
- Maintain updated unsubscribe lists
- Honor opt-out requests immediately
- Send only relevant, expected content
- Identify brand clearly
- Include contact information
- Segment audiences appropriately
- Monitor bounce rates and list hygiene

Users shall not:
- Send to purchased or scraped lists
- Ignore opt-out requests
- Use misleading sender information
- Send to inactive addresses
- Cross-list contacts without consent
- Resell or share subscriber lists

### 5.3 Consent Management
Users responsible for:
- Maintaining documented consent
- Tracking opt-in/opt-out status
- Honoring preference updates
- Implementing double-opt-in
- Preserving consent records
- Proving consent if challenged

## 6. Service Disruption

### 6.1 Prohibited Disruption
Users shall not:
- Launch denial-of-service (DoS) attacks
- Execute distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks
- Overload systems through automated scraping
- Create resource exhaustion attacks
- Use botnet traffic
- Implement SYN floods or ping floods
- Conduct amplification attacks
- Intentionally crash infrastructure

**Enforcement:** Immediate account termination; ISP and law enforcement notification; legal action pursued.

### 6.2 Accidental Overload
- Good-faith errors handled with warning first
- Customer worked with to implement fixes
- Temporary rate limiting applied
- No account termination for honest mistakes
- Emergency contact provided for incidents

## 7. Data Scraping and Extraction

### 7.1 Prohibited Scraping
Users shall not:
- Scrape contact information from Platform
- Extract customer data without authorization
- Use automated tools to harvest email addresses
- Bypass robots.txt or terms restrictions
- Create competing contact databases
- Resell scraped data
- Use scraping to circumvent pricing
- Overload systems through scraping

### 7.2 Permitted Data Access
Users may:
- Export their own data via provided tools
- Access API for authorized integrations
- Use webhooks for event notifications
- Query databases within rate limits
- Create reports and analytics
- Integrate with authorized third-parties

## 8. Infrastructure Abuse

### 8.1 Computational Resource Abuse
Users shall not:
- Use Service for cryptocurrency mining
- Conduct GPU-intensive compute jobs
- Run persistent background processes
- Store large media libraries not related to Service
- Use Service as free cloud storage
- Execute complex algorithms exceeding quota
- Bypass fair usage through multiple accounts

### 8.2 Storage and Bandwidth Abuse
Users shall not:
- Exceed reasonable storage quotas
- Host content unrelated to communication
- Use Service as CDN or file hosting
- Distribute large files beyond business use
- Store backups of other systems
- Host public repositories
- Redistribute bandwidth

### 8.3 Third-Party Infrastructure
Users shall not:
- Proxy traffic through Service
- Use Service to bypass ISP restrictions
- Route traffic for non-authorized purposes
- Resell Service bandwidth or computing
- Create VPN services using Service
- Facilitate unauthorized access

## 9. Account Security Violations

### 9.1 Unauthorized Access
Users shall not:
- Access accounts without authorization
- Use credentials of other users
- Share account login information
- Breach account access controls
- Distribute API keys or tokens

### 11.3 Permanent Termination
For serious violations:

- All data access removed
- Data deleted per policy
